## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is BNWAS a certified Bridge Navigational Watch Alarm System?

**No.** BNWAS is a personal watch-discipline tool inspired by the BNWAS concept. It is **not** certified equipment and does **not** comply with IMO Resolution MSC.128(75), SOLAS Chapter V Regulation 19, or any flag-state carriage requirement. It must **never** be used as a substitute for approved, type-certified BNWAS hardware where regulations require one.

On vessels subject to SOLAS or equivalent national rules, the certified installed BNWAS is the only acceptable means of compliance.

### Who is this app for?

- Yacht and small-craft skippers on long single-handed passages.
- Merchant mariners who want a personal cross-check alongside certified equipment.
- Instructors and cadets studying the BNWAS concept.
- Anyone on a solitary night watch who needs an external prod to stay alert.

### How does it work?

1. Choose an interval: 3, 6, 9, or 12 minutes.
2. Press **START WATCH**. The app counts down silently.
3. When the timer expires, the screen begins flashing red (Stage 1 — visual warning).
4. If you do not acknowledge within one minute, the audible alarm starts (Stage 2).
5. Tap **I'M HERE** at any time — from the app or the lock screen — to reset the timer and start the next cycle.

### Does the alarm work when the phone is locked?

Yes. BNWAS uses the iOS background-audio capability solely to deliver the on-device alarm tone when the iPhone is locked. Now Playing controls appear on the lock screen so you can acknowledge without unlocking.

### Does BNWAS collect any data?

**No.** The app runs entirely on-device. It has no accounts, no analytics, no network requests, no ads, and no third-party SDKs. There is nothing to opt out of because there is nothing being collected.

### What happens on first launch?

You are shown a full-screen disclaimer explaining that BNWAS is a personal tool and not certified safety equipment. You must explicitly accept the disclaimer before the app becomes usable. The disclaimer is shown only once.

### The screen goes dark during a watch. Is that normal?

No — BNWAS keeps the screen awake while a watch is running. If the screen still dims on your device, check that Low Power Mode is off and that no MDM profile is forcing display-off behaviour.

### I tapped I'M HERE on the lock screen but the timer did not reset.

Make sure the Now Playing card from BNWAS is visible (not another audio app). Tap the pause/play button on the BNWAS Now Playing tile — in BNWAS, these buttons are wired as an acknowledgement, not as playback control. If the problem persists, email me with your iOS version and device model.

### Can I change the interval to something other than 3, 6, 9, 12 minutes?

Not in version 1.0. These four presets cover the ranges expected by most watchkeeping practices. If you have a concrete use case for a different interval, please email — I am open to adding presets in a future version.

### How do I report a bug?

Email **[tymur.rudov@icloud.com](mailto:tymur.rudov@icloud.com)** with:

- Your iPhone model.
- Your iOS version (Settings → General → About → Software Version).
- BNWAS version (shown at the bottom of the app — or "1.0" for the initial release).
- A short description of what happened and what you expected.
- If relevant, a screenshot or screen recording.
